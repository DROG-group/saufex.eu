The research and publications generated by SAUFEX encompass a wide range of outputs, including peer-reviewed journal articles, conference papers, and technical reports. These publications disseminate the project’s findings to the global academic and professional community, contributing to the ongoing dialogue on disinformation and cybersecurity. Through these publications, SAUFEX not only shares its innovations but also invites collaboration and further research in the field.

Reports and white papers produced by SAUFEX offer in-depth analyses of disinformation trends, the effectiveness of current countermeasures, and the outcomes of project initiatives. These documents are designed to inform policymakers, researchers, and practitioners about the latest developments in FIMI and provide evidence-based recommendations for action. By disseminating these findings, SAUFEX contributes to the broader discourse on combating disinformation.

Ongoing research within the SAUFEX project focuses on advancing the understanding of FIMI and improving the tools and strategies used to combat it. This section highlights current research projects, upcoming publications, and collaborative efforts that are pushing the boundaries of what is possible in the fight against disinformation. By staying at the forefront of research, SAUFEX ensures that its tools and recommendations are based on the most current and robust evidence.

Surveys and focus groups are essential tools for gathering insights from diverse stakeholders. In the SAUFEX project, these activities are designed to capture perceptions, experiences, and opinions on Foreign Information Manipulation and Interference (FIMI). This data is crucial for understanding the impact of disinformation and informing the development of effective countermeasures. The feedback collected through surveys and focus groups will directly influence policy recommendations and the design of educational resources, ensuring that project outcomes are aligned with real-world needs.

The SAUFEX project provides comprehensive guides and tutorials to help stakeholders navigate complex topics related to disinformation and cybersecurity. These resources are meticulously crafted to be user-friendly, offering step-by-step instructions on using the project's tools, understanding AI applications in countering disinformation, and implementing best practices for data management. By empowering users with the knowledge and skills they need, these guides and tutorials play a crucial role in enhancing resilience against FIMI.

Educational Resources

MOOCs & Online Learning

Educational resources developed under SAUFEX aim to raise awareness and build capacity among various audiences, including policymakers, educators, and civil society members. These resources include lesson plans, workshops, and informational materials that explain the mechanisms of disinformation and offer strategies for prevention and response. Designed to be easily integrated into existing educational frameworks, these materials help foster a well-informed and vigilant public.

Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and online learning modules are key components of the SAUFEX educational strategy. These courses provide accessible, in-depth training on topics such as AI in disinformation detection, the role of media literacy in countering FIMI, and the implementation of resilience frameworks. By offering these resources online, SAUFEX ensures that a wide audience can gain the skills and knowledge needed to effectively combat disinformation, regardless of their geographic location.

Technical Resources

The technical repositories developed within SAUFEX are comprehensive databases containing code, algorithms, and technical documentation related to the project. These repositories include resources like OpenCTI cases, STIX 2.1 extensions, and other frameworks that are vital for understanding and combating FIMI. By making these technical resources available, SAUFEX supports ongoing research and development efforts across the cybersecurity community.

SAUFEX case studies provide real-world examples of the project’s tools and strategies in action. These detailed analyses showcase the implementation and impact of FIMI detection and response frameworks in different contexts, offering valuable lessons and best practices. These case studies not only demonstrate the effectiveness of SAUFEX’s approaches but also serve as educational tools for practitioners looking to adopt similar strategies.

Technical resources within the SAUFEX project include detailed documentation on advanced tools like OpenCTI, STIX 2.1 Extensions, and AI-driven analysis frameworks. These documents provide technical stakeholders with the information they need to effectively deploy and customise these tools in their own environments. By providing in-depth technical resources, SAUFEX ensures that its innovations can be effectively integrated into existing cybersecurity infrastructures.
